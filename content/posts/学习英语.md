## Preposition

a hamster and a box.

what prepositions do is they establish relationships between stuff in place and time so it can show us where things are, when things are, and how things are.

- the box is **under** the hamster
- the box is **over** the hamster

another use for prepositions is we can talk about when things are in relation to each other.

- **Before** the hamster got the hat
- **After** the hamster got the hat.

another use that prepositions can do is they can also help express how something is or how it’s, what its use is.

- This hat is for parties.
- This hat is for wearing at parties.

So what’s the purpose of the hat?

So this is the how and this part is the where.

- That is the hat of the hamster.

That’s what prepositions are in the most basic sense they can help you figure out what the relationship is between two things in time and space or how one thing relates to another.

You should figure out when, where, and how using prepositions.

### Prepositions of time

when something is completed

> The bats come out after the sun goes down.

when it is behind an action.

> Can you take out the garbage before you leave the house ?

When we’re talking about “at” we’re talking about a single moment in time.

> The vampire wakes at 10 p.m.

“By”, this is a really precise end time, but not a very precise beginning time.

> This place had better be clean by 3 p.m. , buddy.
>
> if you say something like that, you’re not especially concerned that the place might be clean before three.

“For” denotes duration. How long something has been going on.

> I’ve been a chef for 40 years.

“In” denotes a bounded duration, which lasts for a specific amount of time, like a limited period.

> In march or In the Middle Ages.

“On” has a specific connotation. It’s something that happens on a specific day.

> On the 4th of July, many Americans watch fireworks and eat encaged meats.

“Since” is kind of like “By” except it’s more about the precision of the start point rather than the end point.

> Since 1974, our company has made nothing but toasters.

“Until” is also precise, but it’s a precise ending time.

> You have until midnight to rescue the Ambassador, break the curse and save Prince Wilbur.
